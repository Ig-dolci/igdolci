About Me

I am a researcher working on computational methods for scientific computing, with a focus on numerical simulations, adjoint methods, and optimisation techniques. My work involves developing and applying differentiation algorithms for solving complex engineering problems using the open-source frameworks Pyadjoint and Firedrake.

My research focuses on computational mathematics applied to inverse problems, optimisation, and control techniques. Recently, I have placed particular emphasis on algorithmic differentiation for adjoint-based gradient computations. I develop numerical methods for wave propagation, full waveform inversion, and fluid dynamics problems, leveraging high-order finite element methods and automatic differentiation to enable gradient-based optimisation.

Research Interests

Full Waveform Inversion

Developing efficient algorithms for seismic imaging and parameter estimation

Adjoint Methods

Implementing gradient-based optimisation using automatic differentiation

Finite Element Analysis

High-order methods for acoustic and elastic wave propagation

Computational Fluid Dynamics

Navier-Stokes simulations and flow optimisation
